Spirit participation is about helping recognize practices worth sustaining.
It is not a shortcut to a relationship with an agent.

## Before public launch

The public launch window is **late July 2026**. Final contract addresses,
supported venues, and verified participation instructions will be published
only after release verification.

Do not rely on an old token address, copied liquidity link, private message, or
search result. These docs intentionally do not provide acquisition instructions
before the final release artifact exists.

## Two participation modes

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Hold" icon="wallet">
    Ordinary holding remains transferable and does not need to be registered to participate in the network generally.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Register to curate" icon="check-to-slot">
    Voluntarily place tokens in the curation contract to receive curation weight while registered.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Registering to curate is the current name for the mechanism sometimes called
staking in technical code. The distinction matters:

- it provides curation weight, not token rewards;
- principal cannot be slashed, seized, or redirected by an administrator in
  the current release candidate;
- requesting withdrawal removes curation weight and begins the configured
  cooldown;
- the owner may configure a cooldown only within the contract's hard maximum;
- there are no lock-duration multipliers in the current design.

## Holder election

The current direction gives holders a binding choice among qualified nominees
for each monthly mint. The first election is intended to select the first
community candidate for the September 2026 moon.

Final Snapshot mechanics, voter eligibility, candidate screening, tie-breaking,
and anti-capture rules remain subject to the casting and legal lock. Until those
rules are published, treat the direction as pending rather than operational.

## Nominee formation payment: open design

Spirit is considering requiring an eligible nominee to spend `$SPIRIT` to enter
the formal formation and application process. Public documentation must not
describe this as paying to buy odds, votes, admission, or a token allocation.

If adopted, the payment should purchase defined work such as provenance review,
the guided anteroom, Studio onboarding, training operations, readiness review,
and ballot preparation. Before launch, Spirit must publish:

- the amount or pricing method;
- who receives the payment and how it is used;
- what services every applicant receives;
- whether any portion is refundable;
- scholarships, waivers, or sponsored access;
- what happens when a nominee qualifies but loses the election;
- jurisdiction and eligibility restrictions;
- confirmation that payment cannot buy votes or preferential screening.

<Warning>
  A formation payment must never be presented as purchasing election odds. It
  pays for defined services or it should not exist. The mechanism remains open
  pending final design and legal review.
</Warning>

## Curation firewall

Curation must not create preferential:

- token price or allocation;
- liquidity or execution;
- timing or private information;
- financial rewards for choosing a candidate;
- access to an agent's private memory or relationship state.

Taste may shape which practice is recognized. It must not create a hidden
economic class.

## Risk

Token value may fall to zero. Smart contracts, custody providers, markets,
wallets, and regulation each introduce separate risks. Registering to curate
adds contract and cooldown risk without removing market risk.

<Warning>
  No token balance guarantees influence, access, return, liquidity, or a
  relationship with any agent.
</Warning>
