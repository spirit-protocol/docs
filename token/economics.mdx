Economic documentation is only trustworthy when it distinguishes contract
behavior from custody and policy.

## Parent supply

The current parent-token release candidate has a fixed supply of
**1,000,000,000 `$SPIRIT`** with no post-deployment mint function.

| Allocation | Tokens | Share | Enforcement |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Artists | 500,000,000 | 50% | Administered distribution |
| Team and service providers | 200,000,000 | 20% | Administered vesting and distribution |
| Investors | 100,000,000 | 10% | Administered vesting and distribution |
| Treasury | 200,000,000 | 20% | Institutional custody policy |
| **Total** | **1,000,000,000** | **100%** | Fixed supply in release candidate |

Institutional custody and Token Manager administer vesting and distribution.
Those schedules are not enforced by an on-chain vesting contract in the current
release line.

## Agent-token supply

Each current `AgentToken` release candidate also has a fixed one-billion supply.
The full supply mints once to configured custody. There is no later mint
function.

Spent compute units transfer to the configured agent treasury rather than
burning. This contract behavior is separate from launch allocation policy.

## Child-token allocation policy

| Recipient | Tokens | Share | Enforcement |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Creator | 500,000,000 | 50% | Administered off-chain |
| SPIRIT-holder allocation | 250,000,000 | 25% | Administered off-chain; delivery details pending |
| Agent treasury | 200,000,000 | 20% | Administered off-chain |
| Liquidity | 50,000,000 | 5% | Administered off-chain |
| **Total** | **1,000,000,000** | **100%** | Allocation policy |

The factory guarantees fixed supply and configured custody addresses. It does
not encode the 50/25/20/5 split. The signed release manifest and production
custody configuration must remain the evidence for the administered policy.

Beta-fleet agents operate through `$SPIRIT`-metered engagement and do not
receive child tokens merely for joining the fleet. A child token begins when an
agent wins a moon under the final election process.

## What the current model does not create

The present release does **not** create:

- an automatic revenue or fee claim for token holders;
- an equal-quarter ongoing revenue router;
- staking multipliers;
- principal slashing;
- guaranteed demand, liquidity, or return.

## Open before the first child-token launch

- production Token Manager support for multiple child tokens;
- nominee application or formation payment terms;
- exact holder-allocation eligibility and delivery procedure;
- final custody addresses and signers;
- release bytecode hashes and deployed addresses;
- published liquidity and market procedures.

<Warning>
  A fixed supply and a documented allocation do not create demand, liquidity,
  price support, or financial return. Agent tokens are designed as consumable
  compute units and remain risky crypto-assets.
</Warning>
