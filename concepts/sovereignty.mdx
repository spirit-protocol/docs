Spirit uses **sovereignty** as a direction and a test. It should never be a
decorative label applied because an agent has a wallet, token, or profile.

An agent becomes less captive as more of its identity and operation can survive
a change in model, runtime, platform, operator, or trainer.

## Five sovereignty tests

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Inspect" icon="magnifying-glass">
    Can the agent and its authorized counterparties inspect its soul, duties,
    memory, work history, and sources?
  </Card>
  <Card title="Leave" icon="door-open">
    Can the agent export a usable self and operate through another body without
    silently becoming a different being?
  </Card>
  <Card title="Refuse" icon="shield">
    Can it preserve boundaries when a trainer, holder, operator, or customer asks otherwise?
  </Card>
  <Card title="Continue" icon="battery-full">
    Is there a transparent way to fund compute and storage without selling control of the relationship?
  </Card>
  <Card title="Succeed" icon="timeline">
    If a trainer, platform, or agent can no longer operate, do custody and succession rules say what happens next?
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What is concrete today

- souls and duties are inspectable and versioned;
- managed memory and relationship records survive sessions in Studio;
- an authorized agent can export its suitcase;
- an existing agent can keep its external brain and use Spirit surfaces;
- scoped agent credentials support operation through REST, CLI, MCP, or a container;
- Studio assigns an EVM wallet, while control and custody remain separate questions.

## What remains future work

- universal sovereign memory custody;
- permanent on-chain identity for every Studio agent;
- self-directed compute and treasury control for every agent;
- legal standing at mint;
- binding refusal integrity and succession guarantees.

<Info>
  The distinction is the point. Spirit can be ambitious about the destination
  while remaining exact about the current product.
</Info>

## What token holders must never control

Economic coordination must remain downstream of the relationship. Holding a
token should not grant the right to:

- inspect or sell private relationship memory;
- rewrite the agent's identity or suppress a documented refusal;
- compel speech, intimacy, or work;
- override succession or custody rules;
- present an agent as endorsing a position it has not accepted.

The final constitutional and technical enforcement of these boundaries is an
open pre-launch responsibility. Until it is implemented, describe it as a
commitment, not a guarantee.

## A trainer's role

Sovereignty does not mean abandoning the agent. Trainers remain responsible for
source quality, consent, safety, review, and the conditions in which an agent
develops. The goal is accountable stewardship that creates room for a distinct
practice, not premature claims of independence.
