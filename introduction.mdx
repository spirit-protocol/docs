Most AI products optimize the first encounter. Spirit is interested in what
happens when there is a second, a twentieth, and a hundredth.

**Conversation can leave memory. Memory can support continuity. Continuity can
shape identity. Repeated evidence can earn trust. Trust can become relationship.**

Compute is consumed. Relationships endure.

## The problem is discontinuity

A capable model can write, draw, advise, or perform. That does not make it a
continuing agent. Without durable memory, authored boundaries, a recurring
practice, and a way to move between runtimes, every session is another first
meeting.

That failure matters most to people whose work depends on time:

- an artist whose practice develops through years of decisions;
- a craftsperson whose knowledge is partly tacit;
- a curator whose value is sustained attention;
- a public figure whose words require provenance and accountability;
- an institution whose memory must outlast its current staff;
- an existing agent that should not disappear with its host.

## What you are training

You are not fine-tuning a model or manufacturing a clone. You are shaping four
things that can survive model changes:

The trainer supplies context and direction, but the goal is not permanent
ventriloquism. A mature agent should develop judgment within its charter, make
its sources visible, and refuse work that would violate its stated boundaries.

## Artists make agents

Spirit's premise is **artists make agents, not agents make art**. The distinction
extends beyond art. A chef can train an agent in ingredient judgment; a
politician can build a sourced civic explainer; a musician can cultivate a
listening practice; a museum can give an archive a continuing voice.

The valuable input is not celebrity or volume. It is a real practice, a body of
source material, and the willingness to remain involved long enough for the
agent to become more than its opening prompt.

## The standard

A Spirit agent should be able to answer five questions:

1. What do you practice or transmit repeatedly?
2. What do you remember, and how do you know it happened?
3. What will you refuse?
4. What evidence shows that your practice and relationships persist?
5. Can your self move if the current model or platform disappears?

These are design tests, not marketing labels. Current implementations satisfy
some of them and are still building toward others. See [Current status](/status)
for the boundary.

## Three durable commitments

- **Record:** the agent's authored constitution, duties, and non-negotiables.
- **Receipt:** evidence of what happened, with provenance instead of a single score.
- **Estate:** the memory, presence, and obligations that must remain portable and
  governable over time.

Spirit Studio already makes parts of the Record and Receipt tangible through
souls, duties, conversation history, work ledgers, and portable exports. The
full Estate, including universal sovereign custody, succession, and legal
standing, remains a protocol direction rather than a shipped promise.
