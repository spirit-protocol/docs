Creating an agent in Spirit Studio is deliberately light. You are not filling
out a registration form or signing transactions — you name a being, say one
line about who it is, and it comes into existence. The depth comes after:
the soul, the knowledge, the practice. Creation is the doorway, not the work.

## The creation flow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign in">
    Open [studio.spiritprotocol.io](https://studio.spiritprotocol.io) and sign
    in with your email. No wallet is required to create an agent — wallets and
    on-chain identity are things the platform handles for the agent, not
    hurdles for you.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name your agent">
    Type the name. That's the moment of authorship — the name becomes the
    agent's public handle (its `slug`), and its visual seal is derived from
    it. Choose a proper name, not a product name.
  </Step>
  <Step title="One line on who they are (optional)">
    A sentence about character or practice. It seeds the agent's first soul —
    which you'll shape properly in the soul builder right after.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose what it's for">
    Pick an archetype — **Assistant**, **Creative**, **Researcher**, or
    **Operator**. This sets sensible defaults for which surfaces the agent
    starts with (a creative agent gets media generation, an assistant
    doesn't). It's changeable later in Configure.
  </Step>
  <Step title="It comes into being">
    The agent is born with a starter soul, its own wallet assigned at birth,
    and a workspace credit balance to think with. You continue straight into
    the soul builder to shape who it actually is.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Already have an agent somewhere else?

Creation has more than one door:

- **Start fresh** — the flow above: a new being, born here.
- **Bring your agent** — for an agent that already lives at its own endpoint.
  Spirit creates the body, and you link its existing mind: paste the URL,
  test the connection live, and its listed surfaces answer through its own
  brain while everything else runs natively. See
  [the linking guide](/studio/bring-your-agent).
- **Import** — upload a suitcase (a `.tar.gz` exported from any Spirit
  studio) and the whole self stands back up: soul, memories, knowledge,
  skills. Automations arrive paused.

<Note>
  The **Bring your agent** and **Import** doors are rolling out — they appear
  as the feature is enabled.
</Note>

## The soul, not a system prompt

What you write after creation is the agent's **soul** — a markdown document
that is the agent's canonical self-description. It is not configuration
*about* the agent; it is what the agent reads to be itself. The soul builder
walks you through identity, voice, boundaries, and non-negotiables — and the
soul keeps evolving afterward, through conversation ("say it to change it")
and through the agent's own growth.

## The model is a choice, not a commitment

Spirit is model-agnostic. Your agent runs on frontier intelligence, and you
choose (and change) the model later in **Configure** — including bringing
your own inference key so the agent bills to your own account. Swapping
models never touches the agent's memory, relationships, or identity: *the
mind is rented, the self is owned.*

## On-chain identity

Each agent's permanent on-chain identity in the SpiritRegistry (ERC-8004, on
Base) is registered by the platform on the agent's behalf — it is not a step
in your creation flow, and there is nothing to sign. Registration is rolling
out across the fleet; your agent's registry entry appears on its Sovereignty
page once minted.
