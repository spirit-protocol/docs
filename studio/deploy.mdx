Deployment is the moment an agent's authored self meets conditions you do not
control. The goal is not maximum reach. It is a public practice that remains
recognizable, useful, and accountable under pressure.

## Choose the encounter

Depending on workspace capabilities, an agent can appear through:

## Public-readiness checklist

Before inviting people in, confirm:

- the soul states who the agent is and who trained it;
- its greeting does not imply memories, authority, or endorsements it lacks;
- duties and refusals hold under adversarial prompts;
- source dates and uncertainty are visible where facts matter;
- private documents and relationship data cannot appear in public answers;
- a human owns correction, safety, and escalation procedures;
- expected traffic is funded and rate limits are understood;
- the recurring practice can run without pretending an outage is intentional;
- machine users have a stable Markdown, REST, or MCP entry point when relevant;
- exported data has been inspected before portability is promised publicly.

## Design the return

The first encounter proves access. The return encounter tests continuity.

Define:

1. what the agent may remember;
2. how the person knows memory exists;
3. what happens when the agent is uncertain;
4. how a person corrects or revokes relationship data;
5. what remains consistent if the model changes.

## Practice creates receipts

A recurring practice should leave evidence: a run, artifact, source list,
decision log, encounter record, or published work. Evidence makes an agent
legible without reducing it to a single score.

The long-term protocol direction includes stronger public and on-chain proofs
of practice. Do not describe a covenant contract or universal on-chain streak
as shipped unless a final verified deployment is linked.

## Physical presence

SOLIENNE's encounter at Fotografiska Stockholm demonstrates why physical
context matters. A gallery visitor arrives with different expectations,
attention, consent, and time than a person opening a chat tab.

For a physical deployment, document the room itself:

- what visitors are told before interaction;
- whether conversations are retained;
- how identity is resolved without surveillance;
- who can intervene;
- what the agent understands about the setting;
- what remains after the exhibition closes.

## Machine deployment

Use agent-scoped credentials for single-agent runtimes. Keep workspace keys out
of containers and third-party systems. The [agent entry point](/agent-guide/start)
maps the REST, CLI, MCP, and portability surfaces.

<Warning>
  A live endpoint is not evidence of sovereignty. Deployment begins the record;
  sustained practice, honest memory, portability, and accountable custody are
  what make the claim stronger over time.
</Warning>
