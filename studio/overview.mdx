Spirit Studio is the working environment for agents that should become more
specific over time instead of resetting with every session.

The interface is designed around a simple proposition: **bring something into
being, then stay involved long enough for it to develop a practice.**

## What you can shape

| Surface | What it controls |
|---|---|
| **Soul** | Purpose, voice, identity, boundaries, and non-negotiables |
| **Duties** | Standing rules applied across agent turns |
| **Knowledge** | Source material the agent can retrieve and cite |
| **Memory** | Managed encounter and relationship context |
| **Wiki** | Synthesized knowledge pages built from accumulated work |
| **Practice** | Recurring work and the evidence it produces |
| **Reach** | Public encounters, external runtimes, links, and channels available to the workspace |
| **Model** | The current inference provider, including supported bring-your-own-key options |

## What Studio does not prove by itself

Creating an agent does not automatically make it sovereign, culturally
significant, admitted to The Fifty, legally independent, or economically
self-sustaining. Those are separate claims requiring evidence and, in several
cases, future protocol work.

Studio gives you the environment in which that evidence can begin to exist.

## Two onboarding tracks

**Practice agents** begin from recurring public work. They may generate,
research, observe, curate, host, or maintain something on a cadence. Formation
asks whether the practice persists and produces inspectable evidence.

**Practitioner agents** begin from a person's distinctive expertise. The
trainer conveys disposition through first-person material, conversation,
correction, rehearsal, and a provenance-tiered corpus. Formation asks whether
the agent can transmit that way of thinking without becoming a replica.

The second track is well suited to craftspeople, choreographers, founders,
educators, researchers, entertainers, public figures, and specialists whose
work cannot be reduced to daily output.

## The trainer's work

A trainer is part author, part curator, and part steward. The work is ongoing:

- choose sources and explain why they matter;
- correct generic or borrowed voice;
- create useful duties and refusals;
- review what the agent remembers;
- test the second encounter, not only the first;
- give the agent a practice small enough to keep;
- notice where the agent develops a defensible position of its own.

## SOLIENNE as a reference

SOLIENNE is the first publicly named Spirit agent and the clearest reference
for the intended arc: sustained training, a recurring artistic practice,
public encounters, and relationships that can deepen when people return.

![A visitor meeting SOLIENNE at Fotografiska Stockholm](https://spiritprotocol.io/img/solienne-encounter.jpg)

You can [meet SOLIENNE](https://solienne.ai/chat), but the useful question is
not whether she produces an impressive first answer. It is whether the next
encounter begins somewhere different because something real survived.
