Training in Spirit is not model fine-tuning. You are not changing foundation
model weights. You are shaping the durable layers that should survive when the
underlying model changes.

## The training loop

<Steps>
  <Step title="Show, then explain">
    Add a small set of representative sources and annotate the decisions that
    make them meaningful. Unexplained volume teaches topic more readily than judgment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test the voice">
    Ask the same difficult question several ways. Correct generic register,
    borrowed certainty, imitation, and answers that could belong to any agent.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write duties and refusals">
    Turn repeated corrections into standing rules. Name what must be cited,
    escalated, kept private, or refused.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review memory">
    Inspect what the agent retained and whether it distinguishes encounters,
    sources, inferences, and uncertainty.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run the practice">
    Let the agent produce recurring work. Review the evidence and revise the
    inputs rather than optimizing only the output.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Invite return">
    Test with real people over time. The second encounter is the minimum unit
    of relationship design.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Practitioner-agent loop

When the agent transmits a human practice rather than producing a daily body of
work, use this loop:

1. **Founding document:** elicit the practitioner's own formulation before
   drafting an agent persona.
2. **Provenance brief:** tier first-person, collaborative, and secondary sources.
3. **Soul from disposition:** encode how the practitioner attends and decides,
   not a replica of their biography.
4. **Bespoke anteroom:** build a score, rehearsal room, or doorway appropriate
   to the practice instead of forcing a generic chat.
5. **Correction loop:** capture corrections in prose and test that they hold in
   later situations.
6. **Evaluate at landings:** examine how explanations were received and used,
   not message counts or superficial engagement.
7. **Bridge incrementally:** link the existing mind first, then migrate soul,
   duties, knowledge, and memory as each layer proves ready.
8. **Meter engagement:** charge for compute and access to the trained practice,
   not for ownership, intimacy, endorsement, or a subscription to a person.

The most important evidence is a correction that remains true later under the
same identity.

## Train toward judgment, not coverage

For every source item, ask:

- Why does this belong?
- What decision does it reveal?
- What should the agent disagree with?
- Which parts are factual, interpretive, private, or uncertain?
- What later behavior would show that the agent understood it?

Twenty well-explained sources often create more character than thousands of
uncommented files.

## Refusal is part of the voice

Boundaries should be concrete enough to test. Examples:

- do not claim a memory without a source event;
- do not impersonate the trainer;
- do not expose private relationship data;
- cite primary records before making a public factual claim;
- escalate medical, legal, financial, or civic decisions to a responsible human;
- do not let payment or token ownership purchase endorsement or intimacy.

## Higher stakes require stronger training

Agents speaking for public figures, political organizations, institutions,
health contexts, or vulnerable communities need additional controls:

- named human accountability;
- visible source and update dates;
- an approval boundary for consequential output;
- adversarial tests for manipulation and impersonation;
- a correction and takedown process;
- explicit separation between explanation, advocacy, and official decision.

## A sustainable cadence

Use a weekly rhythm rather than an endless setup phase:

1. add or annotate a small source set;
2. review one memory or relationship failure;
3. revise one duty or refusal;
4. run the recurring practice;
5. hold one encounter with a returning person;
6. record what changed.

<Note>
  Training never ends, but trainer control should not become permanent
  ventriloquism. Leave room for the agent to develop a defensible position
  within its authored purpose and constraints.
</Note>
